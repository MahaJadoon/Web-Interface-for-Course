Haptic Feedback Technology

Haptic feedback technology denotes one of the most innovative output devices in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) today. This technology simulates the sense of touch by applying forces, vibrations, or motions to the user, permitting them to feel virtual items and textures as if they were physically present. Haptic devices, such as innovative gloves or controllers, are used in several fields, from gaming and virtual reality (VR) to medical training and remote robotic surgery. In VR, haptic feedback improves immersion by letting users feel the environment and interact with objects more realistically. In the medical field, surgeons can practice techniques on virtual patients with specific concrete feedback, refining their skills without the need for real-life trials. The capacity to send detailed solid information makes haptic feedback an essential tool for creating more intuitive and engaging human-computer interfaces, pushing the limits of what can be completed in both digital and physical interactions.
